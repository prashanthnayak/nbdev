CautionSummary of Lesson 2:

Core Philosophy

  • Without spiritual discipline, learning Jyotish is difficult - understanding all charts (past, present, future) requires spiritual support
  • Parashara Jyotish is deeply connected to spirituality (Vishnu Purana, Rigveda)
  • Appreciation of Guru is the first and most important step

Key Practices Introduced

1. Guru Vandana

  • Prayer/worship of the Guru (13 stanzas)
  • Done first thing after morning bath
  • Can be recited in one sitting as daily Guru prayer
  • Traditionally sung at initiations in the parampara

2. Pancha Devatas (Five Deities)

These represent the five tattvas and are worshipped in order:

  1. Ganesha (Prithvi/Earth) - Must be worshipped first; sits in Muladhara chakra
  2. Surya (Agni/Fire) - Establishes energy levels
  3. Vishnu (Akasha/Space) - Grants purity and connection
  4. Devi (Jala/Water) - Brings sustenance and empathy
  5. Shiva (Vayu/Air) - Stops storms in life

Important: Any one of these five can lead to moksha if worshipped properly.

Main Sadhanas for PJC Year 1

Ganesha Sadhana (Primary - 1 Year)

Setup: - Draw Swastika mandala with red powder/paste - Place Ganesha picture at eye level (looking slightly up = sattvic) - Light ghee lamp (represents your home - keep it clean and personal) - Face East or Northeast (or strongest direction in Ashtakavarga of Sun)

Mantra Selection: - Use one of 12 Ganesha names based on YOUR LAGNA (from Sri Narada’s list) - Example: Sagittarius Lagna = “Om Soham balachandraya svaha”

Practice: - Minimum: 108 times daily for 1 year - Rigorous: 15 malas daily (22 malas on Chaturthi) - Start on Chaturthi (Ganesha’s tithi) - Keep pen and paper near Ganesha during sadhana (use that pen for Jyotish notes later)

Purpose: Strengthens Lagna, Jupiter, and Mercury → supports learning and Guru-Shishya connection

Narasimha Sadhana (40 Days)

Preparation: 1. Recite Shanti Mantra (from Rigveda) 2. Bija Pranayama (10 times): done with Pranayama - Inhale: “Om” - Hold: “Hreem” - Exhale: “Shraum” - Use both nostrils

Main Mantra: check slide

  • 1,008 times daily for 40 days
  • Start on Tuesday
  • Can continue after 40 days

Purpose: - Connected to Mangala (Mars) - gives strength (satva) - Removes the 6 Ripus (enemies): Moha, Kama, Lobha, Matsarya, Krodha, Mada - Purifies luminaries, nakshatras, and agni - Strengthens mind for learning negative karmas in Jyotish

Muladhara Chakra Sadhana (Optional but Recommended)

  • Light lamp with “Sri Ram Jai Ram Jai Jai Ram”
  • Drink sweet water (jaggery/brown sugar, NOT white sugar) with “Om Ramachandraya Namaha”
  • Narayana Kavacha procedure
  • Mantra: “Om Hreem Kshraum Hum Phat” - 108 times
  • 40 days

Purpose: Cleanses first three chakras (Muladhara, Swadhisthana, Manipura) related to basic instincts and manifested universe

Practical Guidelines

Mala/Rosary: - Rudraksha recommended for Ganesha - 54 beads preferred (108 also fine) - Don’t touch with index finger (tarjani) - Once dedicated to sadhana, don’t use for other purposes

Diet: Vegetarian recommended but not mandatory for these sadhanas

Timing: - Parampara Mantra (Hare Rama Krishna): First thing upon waking - Other sadhanas: After bath, in the morning

Direction: East or Northeast best (or use Ashtakavarga of Sun for personal direction)

CautionSummary of Lesson 4: Panchanga (Five Limbs of Time)

Opening Context

  • This is part of the Aramba (preparatory) portion of PJC Year 1
  • Aramba introduces many concepts that students will revisit throughout the year
  • Focus is on understanding basic concepts before diving deeper

What is Panchanga?

Pancha = Five, Anga = Limbs

The five fundamental divisions of time that form the foundation of any horoscope. Panchanga must be examined before looking at planetary positions, as it shows the quality of time for that birth.

The Five Limbs:

  1. Vara (Day) - Agni Tatva
  2. Tithi (Lunar day) - Jala Tatva
  3. Karana (Half-tithi) - Prithvi Tatva
  4. Nakshatra (Lunar mansion) - Vayu Tatva
  5. Yoga (Sun-Moon combination) - Akasha Tatva

Benefits of Learning Panchanga

  • Vara - Prolongs life
  • Tithi - Brings prosperity
  • Karana - Gives success in all tasks
  • Nakshatra - Removes sin
  • Yoga - Immunity from disease

1. VARA (The Day)

Connection: Agni (fire), energy levels, physical health

Calculation: Sunrise to next sunrise

Seven Varas: Ravivara (Sunday), Somavara (Monday), Mangalavara (Tuesday), Buddhavara (Wednesday), Guruvara (Thursday), Shukravara (Friday), Shanivara (Saturday)

Analysis: - Check Varesha (lord of the day) placement from Lagna - Also check Varesha placement from Surya

Example Given: Franklin D. Roosevelt - Born Monday (Varesha = Moon) - Moon in 10th house conjunct Mars - Moon has zero Digbala (directional strength) in 10th - Moon-Mars = Gandanta (fire-water conflict) - Moon in 6th from Sun (enemy position) - Result: Polio (health issues related to movement on 4th-10th axis)

Vara Chakra Concepts:

  • 6th graha = enemy (Shatru)
  • 8th graha = eclipse (Grahana) - blocks manifestation

2. TITHI (Lunar Day)

Connection: Jala Tatva, relationships, emotional content, interactions

Calculation: Sun-Moon relationship; 1 tithi = 12 degrees of zodiac

30 Tithis total: 15 in Krishna Paksha (waning), 15 in Shukla Paksha (waxing)

Eight Lords: Surya, Chandra, Mangala, Budha, Guru, Shukra, Shani, Rahu (Ketu has no lordship)

Analysis: Check Tithi lord placement from 4th house

Problematic Tithis:

Rikta Tithis (4th, 9th, 14th) - Most problematic: - Too much emotion, self-centered - Chaturthi - Ganesha (deity) - Navami - Durga (deity) - Chaturdashi - Shiva (deity)

Other Doshas: - Ekadashi (11th) - Addiction/anger issues (Mars/Agni) → fasting advised - Trayodashi (13th) - Wealth problems - Krishna Chaturdashi & Shukla Pratipada - Serious relationship issues (related to Soma’s sin with Tara)

Example Given: Marilyn Monroe - Tithi lord = Venus - Venus in 10th house in Aries (enemy sign) - Zero Digbala - Had relationships but they were problematic

3. DIG CHAKRA (Directional Strength)

Eight directions where grahas have full potential:

  • East (Indra) - Surya - Self-realization
  • Southeast (Agni) - Shukra - Details, art
  • South (Yama) - Mangala - Warrior energy
  • Southwest (Niriti) - Rahu - Chaos
  • West (Varuna) - Shani - Detachment
  • Northwest (Vayu) - Chandra - Movement
  • North (Kubera) - Budha - Wealth, profession
  • Northeast (Ishana) - Guru - Knowledge

Plus: Upward (Ketu/Ananta - Moksha), Downward (Lagna/Brahma - Rebirth)

4. KALA CHAKRA (Time/Blockage Wheel)

Shows which grahas block each direction:

  • Mangala blocks Shukra (Agni corner)
  • Guru blocks Mangala (Yama corner)
  • Budha blocks Rahu (Niriti corner)
  • Shukra blocks Shani (Varuna corner)
  • Chandra blocks Budha (Kubera corner)
  • Rahu blocks Guru (Ishana corner)
  • Ketu blocks Surya (Indra corner)

Key Point: These are pairs that create either debate (Sattvic) or war (Tamasic)

Important Reminders:

  • Panchanga is the first layer - more factors will be added
  • Cannot make final judgments from Panchanga alone
  • Must learn grahas, rashis, bhavas before complete analysis
  • Return to Aramba concepts throughout the year as understanding deepens

CautionSummary of Lesson 6: Katapayadi & Bhava Chakras

1. KATAPAYADI SYSTEM (Numerical Values from Names)

Core Principle: Sanskrit consonants have numerical values (0-9)

Key Rules: - Vowels = 0 (only counted if name starts with vowel) - Use pronunciation, not spelling - Drop incomplete consonants (without following vowel) - Ra/La rule: If consonants joined with R or L, keep only initial consonant - Reverse the number after calculation - Divide by 12 to get remainder = Rashi

Example: Vivekananda - Consonants: Va-Ve-Ka-Na-Na-Da - Values: 4-4-1-0-8 - Number: 44108 → Reversed: 80144 - After dividing by 12 → Remainder = 4 (karka rahsi)

Usage: - Rashi (sign) = interaction with people - House number = inner manifestation

2. ASTRONOMICAL BASICS

Horizon: Where earth and sky meet (observer-dependent)

Key Points: - Udaya Lagna = Eastern horizon (rising point) - Asta Lagna = Western horizon (setting point) - 180° from Udaya - MC (Mid-Heaven/Zenith) = 90° above observer - Ecliptic = Horizontal plane between Sun & Earth

Directional Degrees: - North = 0°/360° - East = 90° - South = 180° - West = 270°

3. BHAVA CHAKRA (House System)

Method: Take Lagna degree ± 15 degrees = 30° house span

Example: Swami Vivekananda (26° Dhanu Lagna) - 1st House = 11° Dhanu to 11° Makara - 2nd House = 11° Makara to 11° Kumbha - And so on…

Result: Planets may shift houses if they’re within 15° of cusp

In Vivekananda’s chart: - Mars shifted from 5th → 4th house - Venus shifted from 2nd → 1st house - Jupiter shifted to 10th house

Significance: When many planets shift = major life transformations

4. BHAVA CHALIT CHAKRA

Starting Point: Uses Bhava Lagna (special lagna, moves 2 hours/sign)

Same process: Bhava Lagna degree ± 15°

Key Difference: - Bhava Chakra starts from Udaya Lagna - Bhava Chalit starts from Bhava Lagna (calculated separately)

5. BIOLOGICAL CLOCK CHART (Practical Application)

Setup: 1. Draw Bhava Chalit Chakra 2. Place 6 AM where Sun is located 3. Place 6 PM in 7th house from Sun 4. Each house = 2-hour period

Planet Activities & Best Times:

Planet Activity Best Time
Surya Spiritual practice, energy level Sun’s house + aspects
Chandra Eating/food Moon’s house + aspects
Mangala Exercise, physical strength Mars house + aspects (4th, 7th, 8th from itself)
Budha Reading, learning, playing Mercury’s house + aspects
Guru Big decisions, digestion, conclusions Jupiter’s house + aspects
Shukra Relationships, indulgence Venus’s house + aspects
Shani Resting (15 min minimum) Saturn’s house + aspects
Rahu NO activity - meditation time Rahu’s house + aspects
Ketu Avoid activities - meditation Ketu’s house + aspects

Important Notes: - Check planet’s aspects (5th, 7th, 9th, 2nd from itself for Rahu) - When planets conjunct = divide time between both activities - Each house = 2 hours, but find at least 15 min for that activity - Movement is anti-zodiacal (Sun moves through houses, not signs)

6. CHART TYPES (Three Representations)

  1. Surya Chakra (East Indian) - for spirituality
  2. Brigu Chakra (North Indian) - for Bhava Chakra
  3. Guru Chakra (South Indian) - for Vastu

Practice tip: Use each chart exclusively for 1-3 months to master all types

Homework: Draw your Bhava Chalit Chakra and create your biological clock timing table!

CautionSummary of Lesson 7: Grahas (Planets) - Introduction

Opening Context

  • This is the first of three webinars on Grahas (planets)
  • Traditionally, Jyotish teaching starts with Chapter 3 (Grahas), not Chapters 1-2 (Creation/Philosophy)
  • Students should study Parashara shlokas at least 3 times to truly digest them
  • Use audio recordings from other batches as supplementary material

CORE DEFINITION: What is a Graha?

Parashara’s Definition (Chapter 3, Shloka 1): Heavenly bodies classified by: 1. Visibility (can we see them?) 2. Movement (do they move?)

  • Moving bodies = Graha (from root “grahana” = to seize/eclipse)
  • Fixed bodies = Nakshatra (fixed stars)

KEY POINT: Grahas have the inherent nature to eclipse the mind - this is their fundamental characteristic.

Why Rahu & Ketu are Grahas (Despite Having No Physical Body)

Question addressed: If we only count visible bodies, why include Rahu/Ketu?

Answer: - They are mathematical points (nodes of the Moon) - They create inverse light/shadow (like closing eyes after seeing sun = black circle) - They represent extremes of the mind: - Rahu = Fully material, maximum desire, wants to consume everything - Ketu = Fully detached, wants nothing, renunciation

Manifestations: - Rahu: Foreigners, different cultures, borders, geographical boundaries, fermentation, alcohol, medicine - Ketu: Outcasts, monks/nuns, spiritual people, those who renounce society - Example given: Man who moved to attic for years, refused all contact = clear Ketu influence

PLANETARY MOTION (Critical Concept)

Three Categories of Motion:

  1. Always Direct (Forward):
    • Surya (Sun) & Chandra (Moon) - the Luminaries
    • Establish dharma, predictable, “one foot after another”
  2. Always Retrograde (Backward):
    • Rahu & Ketu - the Nodes
    • Move opposite to all other planets
    • Cause eclipses when they meet luminaries
  3. Can Be Either (Tara Grahas - 5 Planets):
    • Mangala, Budha, Guru, Shukra, Shani
    • When direct → behave like Luminaries (normal, predictable)
    • When retrograde → behave like Nodes (unpredictable, unfulfilled desires)

RETROGRADE PLANETS (Vakri)

Nature: Planet behaves like Rahu/Ketu

Characteristics: - Not progressing - standing still or pacing - Unfulfilled desire/karma with that planet - Unpredictable, odd behavior - Cannot be trusted (unexpected turns) - May not give results (especially in Prashna/horary)

Strength Implications: - Retrograde = Extremely strong (like nodes) - Has Cheshtabala (directional strength) - Can cause highest grahana (eclipse power)

Prashna (Horary) Rules: - If significator is vakri → Won’t happen (or needs 3 attempts) - 3x multiplier: 3x more effort, 3 attempts, or 3 blessings/children

EIGHT TYPES OF MOTION

5 Direct Motions: 1-5. (Various speeds of forward movement)

3 Retrograde Motions: 1. Vakra - Standard retrograde 2. Anu-vakra - Slowing down in retrograde 3. Kutila - Stationary (about to change direction)

IMPORTANT: Kutila applies to both transitions: - Direct → Retrograde - Retrograde → Direct

WHEN DO PLANETS GO RETROGRADE?

Outer Planets (Mars, Jupiter, Saturn):

Exercise given: Draw blank chart, place Jupiter on Lagna

  • 120° from Sun (5th or 9th house) = Kutila (Stationary)
    • 5th house = Just entering retrograde
    • 9th house = Just finishing retrograde
  • 180° from Sun (7th house) = Highest retrograde motion
  • 6th house = Vakra (Mars nature)
  • 8th house = Anu-vakra (Rahu nature)
  • 4th/10th houses = Direct motion range

Inner Planets (Mercury, Venus): - Requires deep astronomical study (Surya Siddhanta) - Not explained in basic course

CHART EXAMPLE: Pandit Sanjay Rath

Jupiter: Sun in 5th from Jupiter → Jupiter stationary (velocity = 7, very low)

Saturn: Opposite Sun (7th house) → Highest retrograde (velocity = -223%)

Velocity Interpretation: - Minus (-) = Retrograde - Close to 0 = Stationary - High positive = Fast direct motion - High negative = Fast retrograde (double speed)

Note: No exact ranges given for motion types - must observe velocity values

DO WE MOVE RETROGRADE PLANETS IN THE CHART?

NO! Planet stays where it is.

Aprakasha Grahas are five invisible celestial points (not physical planets) that can eclipse or disturb the five Pancha Tattwas (elements). They are:

Definition & Scope - Sutika means circumstances related to birth (suta = child, ka = creation) - This is introductory level - full analysis requires divisional charts (D9, D7, D8) - Modern hospital births make some classical observations difficult

Key Charts for Birth Analysis - Rashi (D1) lagna: Most important for birth circumstances - Navamsha (D9) lagna: Shows problems/complications during delivery - Strong D1 lagna = normal delivery; afflicted D9 lagna = complications

Navamsha Lagna Afflictions - Shani (Saturn): Slow, painful delivery - Mars: Surgical intervention, cesarean, tools used - Ketu: Forceps, vacuum extraction, instruments - Rahu/Ketu: Umbilical cord problems; Rahu can show extra membrane on baby

Birth Timing Karakas (Day vs Night birth) - Day birth: Surya = father, Shukra = mother - Night birth: Shani = father, Chandra = mother - If karaka is in 1st, 7th house or on D9 lagna, that parent prayed for the child’s birth - Stronger karaka connection shows which parent’s place/lineage the birth occurred in

Analyzing Birth Circumstances 1. Check which karaka (mother/father) has better connection to lagna 2. Examine 5th house from D9 lagna in D1 chart (rashi from navamsha technique) 3. Check Moon/Venus in D1 for mother’s circumstances 4. Lagna lord afflictions = birth complications

Breech/Abnormal Birth: Lagna rising with tail (certain signs) + afflicted lagna lord

Part 2: Nisargika Dasha (Natural Age Periods)

The Natural Life Cycle (108 years total) - Moon (0-1): Complete dependence on mother - Mars (1-3): Learning individuality, fighting for self - Mercury (3-12): Learning language, knowledge; gender doesn’t matter - Venus (12-32): Material life, relationships, earning, comfort - first guru (Shukracharya) - Jupiter (32-50): Responsibilities, saving money, children - second guru - Sun (50-70): Learning to give up, delegate - Saturn (70+): Renunciation, ashram life

Key Concepts - 108 years = physical maximum (9 signs × 12 = 108) - 120 years (Vimshottari) = gift from rishis - Extension beyond 108 requires karmic blessings - Shows how aligned we are with natural life progression

Part 3: Calculating Nisargika Antardasha

Step 1: Assign Weights Based on Drishti

From mahadasha lord as “lagna”: - 3rd & 10th houses: 3/4 of dasha - 4th & 8th houses (Mars aspect): 1/4 of dasha
- 5th & 9th houses (Jupiter aspect): 1/3 of dasha - 7th house: 1/7 of dasha - 2nd, 6th, 11th, 12th houses (no drishti): Full (1) dasha - Dasha lord itself: 1 - Nodes (Rahu/Ketu): Not used

Step 2: Calculate ‘M’ Value - Sum all weights: (1 + 1/3 + 1/3 + 1 + 1/7 + 1 + 1) = M - Multiply by mahadasha years (e.g., Jupiter = 18 years) - Convert to years, months, days

Step 3: Antardasha Order

  1. Kendras (strongest to weakest): 10th → 7th → 4th → 1st
  2. Panapharas (from 5th house perspective): 2nd → 11th → 8th → 5th
  3. Apoklimas (from 9th house perspective): 6th → 3rd → 12th → 9th

When Multiple Planets in Same House: - Exalted planet first - Then Mulatrikona - Then own sign (swarashi) - Then lower degree before higher degree (natural progression) - Exalted planets DON’T exchange in parivartana

Part 4: Using Nisargika Dasha

What It Shows - How aligned you are with natural life progression - Why certain life events happen at unusual ages (e.g., marriage in Jupiter dasha instead of Venus) - Starting point for understanding life timing

Practical Example (Teacher’s chart) - Venus mahadasha, Sun antardasha: Life-threatening car accident - Venus = 6th lord (accidents) in 7th (marakasthana, vehicles) - Sun = conjoined Ketu (badakesha), afflicted by malefics - Sun-Mercury exchange (Utpanna principle) = Sun gives Mercury’s results

  • Venus-Mercury antardasha: Started learning Jyotish
    • Venus = 11th lord (formal occult study)
    • Mercury = 9th lord exalted in 5th (learning)
    • Sun-Mercury exchange = Mercury gives Sun’s excellent results
  • Mars antardasha: Marriage (Mars = 12th lord, dispositor of 7th lord)

Important Principles - Use 3 dasha systems together: Vimshottari, Rashi dasha, Longevity dasha - Mahadasha lord placement shows if natural timing is supported - Antardasha shows specific event triggers - Utpanna (exchange) principle crucial for timing
